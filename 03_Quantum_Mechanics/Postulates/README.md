# Postulates of Quantum Mechanics

## Overview

Postulates are foundational, unproven assumptions used as starting points for a theoretical framework. These postulates must be verifiable through experiments. For example, the First Law of Thermodynamics states that energy cannot be created or destroyed. The Second Law of Thermodynamics states that the total entropy of an isolated system always increases over time. Therefore, postulates are assumptions that are verified experimentally.
