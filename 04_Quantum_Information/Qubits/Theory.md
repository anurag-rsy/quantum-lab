A qubit (quantum bit) is the quantum counterpart to a bit in classical computing. Unlike a classical bit, which can only be a 0 or a 1, a qubit can exist as both 0 and 1 at the same time. The primary properties of qubits are superposition and entanglement. Qubits remain in this simultaneous state until they are measured. When qubits are entangled, measuring one instantly provides information about the other. Upon measurement, the fluid quantum state collapses into a definite classical state.
